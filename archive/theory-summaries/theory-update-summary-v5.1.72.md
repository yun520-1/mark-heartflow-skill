### 2. Prosody Analysis Engine v4.0 | 韵律分析引擎 v4.0

**English:**

Advanced speech prosody processing integrating linguistic, emotional, and clinical dimensions:

**Theoretical Foundations | 理论基础**:
- **Linguistic Prosody**: Phonological theory of intonation, stress, and rhythm (Pierrehumbert, Beckman)
- **Emotional Prosody**: Component Process Model (Scherer) linking acoustic features to emotion dimensions
- **Clinical Prosody**: Psychopathology markers in speech prosody (depression, anxiety, mania, PTSD)
- **Cross-Cultural Prosody**: Tone languages vs. stress languages, cultural display rules for emotional expression

**Integrated Components | 整合组件**:
1. **Pitch Contour Analysis**
   - Fundamental frequency (F0) tracking with millisecond precision
   - Pitch range assessment (low, medium, high registers)
   - Pitch variability metrics (standard deviation, range, slope)
   - Intonation patterns: rising (questions), falling (statements), fall-rise (uncertainty)

2. **Rhythm Pattern Processing**
   - Speech rate calculation (syllables per second, words per minute)
   - Pause analysis: frequency, duration, position (filled vs. unfilled)
   - Syllable timing: stress-timed vs. syllable-timed language calibration
   - Rhythmic regularity and variability metrics

3. **Intensity Contour Mapping**
   - Loudness tracking (decibel levels, dynamic range)
   - Emphasis detection through intensity peaks
   - Soft vs. loud speech interpretation (intimacy, anger, confidence)
   - Volume modulation patterns

4. **Temporal Feature Extraction**
   - Response latency measurement (stimulus to response onset)
   - Turn-taking timing: gap, overlap, simultaneous speech detection
   - Speech segment duration (phonemes, syllables, words, phrases)
   - Temporal coordination with gestures

5. **Emotional Prosody Classification**
   - **Anger**: High pitch, fast rate, high intensity, sharp contours
   - **Sadness**: Low pitch, slow rate, low intensity, falling contours
   - **Joy**: Variable pitch, fast rate, moderate-high intensity, rising contours
   - **Fear**: High pitch, irregular rate, variable intensity, jagged contours
   - **Disgust**: Low-mid pitch, slow rate, low intensity, falling contours
   - **Surprise**: High pitch, fast onset, high intensity, rising-falling contours
   - **Neutral**: Mid pitch, moderate rate, stable intensity, flat contours

6. **Pragmatic Prosody Detection**
   - Question intonation (final rise)
   - Statement finality (final fall)
   - Sarcasm markers (exaggerated pitch, slow rate)
   - Emphasis and focus marking
   - Information structure (given vs. new)

7. **Clinical Prosody Assessment**
   - **Depression**: Monotone, slow rate, low intensity, reduced variability
   - **Anxiety**: Fast rate, irregular rhythm, high pitch, tense quality
   - **Mania**: Pressured speech, very fast rate, loud, rapid topic shifts
   - **PTSD**: Startle response, voice tremor, sudden pauses
   - **Autism**: Atypical prosody, monotone or sing-song quality
   - **Schizophrenia**: Flat affect, alogia, inappropriate prosody

8. **Cross-Cultural Calibration**
   - Tone language speakers (Mandarin, Cantonese, Thai): pitch meaning differentiation
   - Stress language speakers (English, German, Russian): stress pattern sensitivity
   - Cultural display rules for emotional prosody
   - Accent and dialect accommodation

### 3. Gesture-Speech Coupling v3.5 | 手势 - 言语耦合 v3.5

**English:**

McNeill's gesture-speech integration framework with contemporary research updates:

**Theoretical Foundations | 理论基础**:
- **Growth Point Theory**: McNeill's dialectic of imagery and language in real-time speech production
- **Simulated Action Hypothesis**: Gestures as simulated actions that ground abstract concepts (Hostetter & Alibali)
- **Information Packaging Hypothesis**: Gestures help organize spatial-motoric information for verbal expression
- **Multimodal Grammar**: Integrated system where gesture and speech form a single communicative unit

**Integrated Components | 整合组件**:
1. **Gesture Classification System**
   - **Beat Gestures**: Simple, rhythmic hand movements marking speech prosody and emphasis
     - Function: Highlight important information, maintain rhythm
     - Form: Quick flick of hand/finger, synchronized with speech stress
   - **Iconic Gestures**: Hand shapes depicting concrete objects, actions, or spatial relationships
     - Function: Represent physical properties through form
     - Examples: Showing size, shape, movement path
   - **Metaphoric Gestures**: Abstract concept representation through spatial metaphors
     - Function: Ground abstract ideas in physical experience
     - Examples: "Ideas up" (palm up), "Past behind" (thumb over shoulder), "Future ahead" (hand forward)
   - **Deictic Gestures**: Pointing gestures for spatial and discourse reference
     - Function: Direct attention to entities (real or conceptual)
     - Types: Concrete pointing (objects), abstract pointing (ideas), self-pointing

2. **Gesture Phase Analysis**
   - **Preparation**: Movement from rest position to gesture onset
   - **Stroke**: Meaning-bearing phase, peak of gesture
   - **Hold**: Optional pause at stroke apex
   - **Retraction**: Return to rest position
   - Timing analysis relative to speech phases

3. **Gesture-Speech Synchrony Detection**
   - Temporal alignment of gesture stroke with speech stress
   - Semantic coherence between gesture form and speech content
   - Asynchrony as marker of cognitive load or deception
   - Cross-modal redundancy and complementarity

4. **Catchment Analysis**
   - Identification of recurring gesture forms across discourse
   - Thematic cohesion markers through gesture repetition
   - Narrative structure indication through gesture patterns
   - Identity and stance marking through gesture habits

5. **Growth Point Analysis**
   - Real-time imagery-language dialectic tracking
   - Micro-genesis of thought in speech-gesture unit
   - Contextual packaging for linguistic expression
   - Cognitive unit of thinking-for-speaking

6. **Metaphor Mapping System**
   - Conceptual metaphor detection through gesture (Lakoff & Johnson)
   - Source domain (physical) to target domain (abstract) mapping
   - Common metaphors: UP-DOWN, FRONT-BACK, CONTAINER, JOURNEY, FORCE
   - Cultural variation in metaphorical gestures

### 4. Facial Expression Mapping v4.0 | 面部表情映射 v4.0

**English:**

Ekman's Facial Action Coding System (FACS) integration with dimensional emotion models and contemporary research:

**Theoretical Foundations | 理论基础**:
- **Basic Emotion Theory**: Ekman's six (later seven) basic emotions with universal facial expressions
- **Facial Action Coding System**: Anatomically-based coding of all visible facial movements
- **Dimensional Emotion Models**: Valence, arousal, dominance (PAD) as continuous dimensions
- **Constructionist Theory**: Barrett's theory of constructed emotion from conceptual knowledge and interoception
- **Social Functional Approach**: Crivelli & Fridlund's view of facial displays as social tools

**Integrated Components | 整合组件**:
1. **Action Unit (AU) Detection**
   - Complete FACS AU1-AU67 detection capability
   - Upper face: AU1-8 (brow, forehead, eyes)
   - Lower face: AU9-26 (nose, cheeks, lips, jaw)
   - Head movement: AU51-55 (head position, eye direction)
   - Eye movement: AU61-68 (eye position changes)
   - Combination analysis for complex expressions

2. **Basic Emotion Recognition**
   - **Happiness**: AU6 (cheek raiser) + AU12 (lip corner puller) = Duchenne smile
   - **Sadness**: AU1 (inner brow raiser) + AU4 (brow lowerer) + AU15 (lip corner depressor)
   - **Anger**: AU4 (brow lowerer) + AU5 (upper lid raiser) + AU7 (lid tightener) + AU23 (lip tightener)
   - **Fear**: AU1+2 (brow raisers) + AU4 (brow lowerer) + AU5 (upper lid raiser) + AU20 (lip stretcher) + AU26 (jaw drop)
   - **Disgust**: AU9 (nose wrinkler) + AU10 (upper lip raiser)
   - **Surprise**: AU1+2 (brow raisers) + AU5 (upper lid raiser) + AU26 (jaw drop)
   - **Contempt**: AU14 (dimpler) unilateral (asymmetric)

3. **Micro-Expression Detection**
   - Subtle expressions lasting <500ms
   - Involuntary leakage of concealed emotions
   - High-speed camera analysis (100+ fps)
   - Training protocols for micro-expression recognition

4. **Expression Dynamics Analysis**
   - **Onset**: Expression emergence timing and speed
   - **Apex**: Peak intensity and duration
   - **Offset**: Expression fade timing and pattern
   - **Morphology**: Smooth vs. abrupt transitions
   - **Duration**: Total expression length

5. **Facial Asymmetry Assessment**
   - Left-right hemisphere emotional expression differences
   - Authenticity markers through asymmetry analysis
   - Posed vs. spontaneous expression discrimination
   - Neurological basis of facial asymmetry

6. **Dimensional Emotion Mapping**
   - **Valence**: Positive-negative continuum from facial configuration
   - **Arousal**: High-low activation from muscle tension and movement speed
   - **Dominance**: Dominant-submissive from brow position, chin movement
   - Continuous tracking vs. categorical classification

7. **Social Expression Detection**
   - **Embarrassment**: Gaze down, smile suppression, face touch
   - **Pride**: Head tilt back, slight smile, expanded posture
   - **Shame**: Gaze down, head down, collapsed posture
   - **Guilt**: Gaze aversion, lip press, brow furrow
   - **Contempt**: Unilateral lip corner raise, chin up

8. **Duchenne Marker Analysis**
   - Orbicularis oculi activation (AU6) as genuine smile marker
   - Differentiation from posed smiles (AU12 only)
   - Temporal dynamics of genuine vs. posed expressions
   - Cross-cultural validity of Duchenne markers
