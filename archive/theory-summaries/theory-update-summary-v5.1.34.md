### 1. Cross-Cultural Emotion Theory | 跨文化情绪理论

**English:**

**Source**: SEP Emotion §6 + Ekman (1992) + Barrett (2017) + Cultural Constructionism

**Key Components:**

1. **Basic Emotion Universality**
   - Six basic emotions: happiness, sadness, fear, anger, surprise, disgust
   - Cross-cultural facial expression recognition
   - Biological basis: limbic system activation patterns

2. **Cultural Specificity**
   - Culture-specific emotion concepts (e.g., Japanese "amae", German "Schadenfreude")
   - Cultural display rules: when/how emotions can be expressed
   - Appraisal patterns vary by cultural context

3. **Constructionist Approach**
   - Emotions constructed from core affect + cultural concepts
   - Language shapes emotion categorization
   - Cultural scripts guide emotion experience

**Integration Points:**
- Links to: Emotion Prototype Theory, Appraisal Theory, Emotion Regulation
- Enables: Culturally-adaptive emotion labeling, culturally-sensitive interventions

### 2. Attachment Theory | 依恋理论

**English:**

**Source**: Bowlby (1969, 1973, 1980) + Ainsworth (1978) + Main & Solomon (1990) + Mikulincer & Shaver (2007)

**Key Components:**

1. **Four Attachment Styles**

     },
     avoidant: {

     },

2. **Internal Working Models**
   - Self-model: Am I worthy of love and support?
   - Other-model: Are others available and responsive?
   - Formed in early caregiving, updated through relationships

3. **Attachment & Emotion Regulation**
   - Secure: Flexible regulation, seeks support when needed
   - Anxious: Hyperactivation (amplify distress to elicit care)
   - Avoidant: Deactivation (suppress attachment needs, self-reliance)
   - Disorganized: Collapse of regulation strategies

4. **Adult Attachment in Romantic Relationships**
   - Attachment orientations influence partner selection
   - Attachment security can change through relationships
   - Therapy can shift attachment toward security

**Integration Points:**
- Links to: Emotion Regulation, Self-Consciousness, Collective Intentionality, Empathy
- Enables: Attachment-informed interventions, relationship pattern analysis, security-building exercises

### 3. Cultural Display Rules | 文化展示规则

**English:**

**Source**: Ekman & Friesen (1969) + Matsumoto (1990) + Cultural Psychology

**Key Components:**

1. **Display Rule Types**
   - **Intensification**: Exaggerate expression (e.g., smiling more at gifts)
   - **De-intensification**: Mute expression (e.g., suppressing grief at work)
   - **Neutralization**: Show no expression (e.g., poker face)
   - **Masking**: Show different emotion (e.g., smiling when disappointed)
   - **Simulation**: Show emotion not felt

2. **Cultural Variations**
   - Individualistic cultures: More direct expression of ego-focused emotions (anger, pride)
   - Collectivistic cultures: More suppression of disruptive emotions, emphasis on harmony
   - Power distance: Expression varies by status of interaction partner

3. **Gender Display Rules**
   - Men: Encouraged to suppress vulnerability (sadness, fear)
   - Women: Encouraged to suppress anger, express warmth

**Integration Points:**
- Links to: Cross-Cultural Emotion, Emotion Regulation, Social Psychology
- Enables: Culturally-appropriate emotion interpretation, avoids misreading display rules as genuine emotion

**中文:**

**来源**: Ekman & Friesen (1969) + Matsumoto (1990) + 文化心理学

**核心组件:**

1. **展示规则类型**
   - 强化、弱化、中性化、掩饰、模拟

2. **文化变异**
   - 个人主义文化：更直接表达自我聚焦情绪（愤怒、自豪）
   - 集体主义文化：更多压抑破坏性情绪，强调和谐
   - 权力距离：表达因互动对象地位而异

3. **性别展示规则**
   - 男性：被鼓励压抑脆弱（悲伤、恐惧）
   - 女性：被鼓励压抑愤怒，表达温暖

**集成点:**
- 链接至：跨文化情绪、情绪调节、社会心理学
- 实现：文化适当的情绪解读，避免误读展示规则为真实情绪
