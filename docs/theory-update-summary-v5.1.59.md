## Theoretical Foundations | 理论基础

### 1. SEP Emotion Theory Three Traditions | SEP 情绪理论三大传统

**English:**

Based on SEP Emotion §2 (Scarantino 2016), emotions can be categorized into three broad theoretical traditions:

| Tradition | Core Claim | Key Theorists | Integration Status |
|-----------|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Feeling Tradition** | Emotions are distinctive conscious experiences | James (1884), Lange (1885), Mandler (1990) | ✅ Integrated v5.1.59 |
| **Evaluative Tradition** | Emotions involve distinctive evaluations of circumstances | Aristotle, Stoics, Nussbaum, Solomon | ✅ Integrated v5.1.59 |
| **Motivational Tradition** | Emotions are distinctive motivational states | Descartes, Darwin, Frijda | ✅ Integrated v5.1.59 |

**Key Theoretical Challenges Addressed:**
- **Differentiation**: How emotions differ from each other and from non-emotional states
- **Motivation**: Whether and how emotions motivate behavior
- **Intentionality**: Whether emotions have object-directedness and appropriateness conditions
- **Phenomenology**: Whether emotions always involve subjective experiences

**中文:**

基于 SEP 情绪理论第 2 节 (Scarantino 2016)，情绪可分为三大理论传统：

| 传统 | 核心主张 | 关键理论家 | 整合状态 |
|------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **感觉传统** | 情绪是独特的意识体验 | 詹姆斯 (1884)、兰格 (1885)、曼德勒 (1990) | ✅ 已整合 v5.1.59 |
| **评估传统** | 情绪涉及对环境的独特评估 | 亚里士多德、斯多葛、努斯鲍姆、所罗门 | ✅ 已整合 v5.1.59 |
| **动机传统** | 情绪是独特的动机状态 | 笛卡尔、达尔文、弗里达 | ✅ 已整合 v5.1.59 |

**解决的关键理论挑战:**
- **区分性**: 情绪如何彼此区分以及与非物质状态区分
- **动机性**: 情绪是否以及如何促进行为
- **意向性**: 情绪是否具有对象指向性和恰当性条件
- **现象学**: 情绪是否总是涉及主观体验

### 2. SEP Self-Consciousness Dual-Layer Model | SEP 自我意识双层模型

**English:**

Based on SEP Self-Consciousness (Zahavi 2005, 2007; Heidelberg School), self-awareness operates at two levels:

| Layer | Description | Key Features | Integration Status |
|-------|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Pre-reflective** | Immediate, non-objectifying self-awareness | First-personal givenness, minimal self, ownership sense | ✅ Enhanced v5.1.59 |
| **Reflective** | Thematic, objectifying self-consciousness | Explicit self-knowledge, autobiographical reasoning, narrative identity | ✅ Enhanced v5.1.59 |

**Historical Lineage:**
- **Ancient**: Aristotle (De Anima), Avicenna (Flying Man argument), Aquinas
- **Early Modern**: Descartes (cogito), Locke (intuitive knowledge), Hume (bundle theory), Kant (transcendental apperception)
- **Post-Kantian**: Fichte (immediate self-positing), Hegel, Heidelberg School (Henrich, Frank)
- **Contemporary**: Zahavi (pre-reflective self-consciousness), Sartre (non-thetic awareness)
